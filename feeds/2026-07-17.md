💬 [@b0rk](https://social.jvns.ca/@b0rk) You may be able to speed up writes by running `PRAGMA synchronous=normal` (per-connection setting). By default, SQLite does a full fsync on each commit, so it persists even if the OS crashes, but this usually isn't a necessary precaution for WAL. WAL guarantees that even with `PRAGMA synchronous=normal`, it won't corrupt data if the OS crashes, but some transactions may be lost.

(On my desktop, this has an approximately 100x difference in commit rates.)
