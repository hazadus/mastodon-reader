**🕒 19:37 👤 @haitchfive@oldbytes.space**
💬 slabbis: performance report

A benchmark analysis comparing slabbis in-process, slabbis-RESP, and Redis across GET, SET, DEL, EXISTS, Mixed 80/20, MGET, and MSET — measured on Apple M1.

[https://github.com/ha1tch/slabbis/blob/main/perf/report.pdf](https://github.com/ha1tch/slabbis/blob/main/perf/report.pdf)

The headline: in-process embedding is 60–80× faster than any TCP-based cache including Redis. That gap is structural — it's the round-trip, not the cache. No amount of server-side optimisation closes it.

At the RESP level, slabbis and Redis are effectively at parity on simple operations. MGET and MSET favour slabbis after the v0.1.2 pool strategy eliminated per-request allocation on the read path.

The persistent tail latency gap (~2× p999 vs Redis) is the honest cost of Go's GC versus C's manual memory management. It shows up at p999, not p50 or p99, and is bounded and predictable.

Report includes five charts (throughput, RESP comparison, before/after v0.1.1→v0.1.2, latency percentile profiles, in-process speed-up). Source in perf/ — regenerate with make all.
