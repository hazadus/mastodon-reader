Tonight was the first memorably "go use the command line, kid" moment.

As I've previously mentioned, I treat Linux Mint like a server, because to me, everything is a server.

In particular, I SSH into my Desktop more often than I sit down at the keyboard. 

Rarely, this results in my finding that my second harddrive has not been mounted, because it is waiting for me to click on it. 

What is very unusual for Mint, is that there doesn't seem to be any way to remove this required click, without resorting to editing `/etc/fstab`.

So I did, after reading some forums. 

I learned that `ls /dev/disk/by-label` could give me a hint how my disk was previously mounted.

And `df -x squashfs -x tmpfs` gives a quick look at the interesting disks.
